Work break down structure

Introduction

One of the initial steps involved in a project are defining of a project scope, work and organization breakdown structures and then estimating the cost and time of each work package in the cost accounts. A project manager can be able work in a systematic way to yield maximum output possible. The purpose of project one was to give us the necessary skills to chalk out these technical aspects of project management.

 

In this project, defining the project of a local stage play was under consideration. This report clearly answers those questions of the project. There are three parts of the question:

  • The first part of the question embodies the final work breakdown structure of a local stage play and comprehensive description of each of the deliverables along with the description of coding scheme practiced.
  • Then in the second part of the question final organization breakdown structure and clear illustration of each of the role’s responsibility. Also an integration of OBS and WBS will be shown.
  • In the third part an example of a work package is given to present the idea of a work package.

This report also contains the performance grading of the team. Finally, report will be concluded with the highlights of the project 1, reason behind the process used and improvements which could have been useful along with the management strategy.

One important thing to mention here is that the team made some assumptions to be more specific in defining the project. Finally, the assumption statement is:

“The local stage play will be a local issue awareness event for the community which should have to be completed within one month.”

Project 1 had some technical aspects of the project management, so I decided with team consensus to do extra training sessions to keep all the team at one page and hinder ambiguous concepts. This was an extra effort of the team which kept everyone in the right direction.

Work Breakdown Structure

Part – 1

Solution – 1:

 

“Develop a WBS for a local stage play. Be sure to identify the deliverables. How would you code your system?”

 

There are three parts in question 1:

1  – Final WBS of local stage Play:

The first part of the solution contains the final work breakdown structure of local stage play devised by the team. Below is shown the final WBS:

1  – Description of Deliverables

The deliverables of the local stage play are categorized in three main deliverables.

1.1  - Play activities

All the tasks and work required to design and perform the stage play are put into play activities. These activities include the following:

      1.1.1 - Stage Play administration: This kind of work includes the proper management of all the activities which will be performed on the stage including the management of stage area and backstage area. There are three categories of stage play administration.

      1.1.1.1 - Event scheduling involves the scheduling of all the activities

to be carried out at the stage while the actual event is happening. This includes the proper time allocation to each performance and hierarchy of events.

      1.1.1.2 – Communication is an important part of the stage

administration which must be efficient to smoothly carry out all the acts.

      1.1.1.3 – Handling Backstage covers the management of backstage

area so that all the important things required during the performance are at their right place and in case of any equipment problem troubleshooting team is ready for quick response.

      1.1.2 – Script writing is the one of the first steps involved in the project of a local stage play. Script of the play must be coherent with the overall theme of the event and demonstrated the main conception of the play without any ambiguity.

      1.1.3 – Set Designing: The background and overall set design of the play presents the general theme of the play and creates the environment to flow the feelings of the script through performance. Set designing is done in two parts.

      1.1.3.1 – Construction of the set is providing the basic structure of the

whole background.

      1.1.3.2 – Prop Designing includes the designing of gadgets to be used

by performers or actors such as: swords, arrows etc.

      1.1.4 – Costume Crafting includes the designing of clothing, footwear and other garments.

      1.1.5 – Light/Sound Work: These includes the operation of the lights and the sound devices in conjunction with the performers in order to enhance the act.

      1.1.6 – Makeup of the actors includes the facial alterations as well as hair styles

            o   1.1.7 – Acting is the soul of the whole event. The quality of acting will determine how successful event was?

 

1.2  – Event support

Event support are the activities which are required to handle all the tasks of the event other then play related. These activities are required to keep discipline and to ease the audience during the actual event is happening. These include the following:

      1.2.1 – Security of the event is one of the prime concerns of the audience, organizers and local government. The area of the event should be kept secure and well maintained before the event to curb any unwanted events. For this purpose, security is done in two steps.

      1.2.1.1 – Crowd is controlled by using the volunteer force to manage

the audience seating and keep the necessary discipline in the audience.

      1.2.1.2 – Security Inspections of the whole area should be done

before the event and also security patrols are must to ensure the safety of the event and all the audience.

      1.2.2 – Ticket Checking must be done in order to ensure that everyone has provided their details and also to make certain that the audience doesn’t exceed the maximum seating limit of the area.

      1.2.3 – Help Desks are set up at different locations of the event in order to provide necessary help and information to audience and to ease their way to the area.

      1.2.4 – Logistic Collection includes the tasks which are required to ensure that the all the equipment which is needed for the event is made available at a particular time and place. This includes buying and collection of two kinds of logistics:

      1.2.4.1 – Set Material required for the construction of the set and all

the other props.

      1.2.4.2 – Other equipment includes seating arrangement for

audience, availability of water or drinks, audio/video devices etc.

 

1.3  – Production

Production of a local stage play monitors the big picture and it usually involves providing the main support for the whole event before the event. This can be categorized into three types:

      1.3.1 – Finance Management covers all the decisions and the financial help required for the event. This activity usually brings and attracts sponsors for the events by using the contacts and also allocating budget to different type of tasks according to the priority and significance of that work. That’s how this activity is subdivided into 1.3.1.1 and 1.3.1.2.

 

 

      1.3.2 – Government Support is typically needed to get approval of the local stage play if required and also help of police for ensuring traffic control and security of the event.

      1.3.3 – Marketing/Promotion of the event is required to spread the word about the venue, time and purpose of the event. This can be achieved by two mediums.

        1.3.3.1 Posters/Flyers should be printed and displayed/distributed

among the local community.

        1.3.3.2 Advertisements through newspapers and the local TV channels

will be periodically broadcasted.

3 – Coding technique

Deliverables of the work breakdown structure are coded as shown in the final WBS in a very concise way. The whole project of the local stage play is represented by code “1”. Then the main sub deliverables of Local stage play are put in level 2 and are coded as 1.1,

1.2  and 1.3. Hence, the sub sub deliverables in the level 3 are coded according to their parent sub deliverable in level 2 by putting extensions of numbers in front of them like 1.1.1, 1.1.2, 1.1.3 etc. Respectively all the sub deliverables are given the codes till level 4. The coding of the cost account and a work package will be briefly described in upcoming sections of the question.

Organization breakdown structure

Part – 2

“Develop a corresponding OBS which identifies who is responsible for what?”

Solution 2:

This question demands two things, first one is to develop a corresponding organization breakdown structure and the second demand is to identify who is responsible for what? The second demand can be fulfilled by providing integration of OBS and WBS which yields cost accounts and work packages and then it will be easy to figure out the person or team responsible for a particular work package.

 

Final corresponding OBS

After the deliverables are figured out in WBS it was easy to determine what kind of team is required for a local stage play. The only thing was to group that team in a way that describes clear role of each of the person and team responsible and also who will be working under whom or collaborating with which team or authority. The final OBS is presented below. You can see that there are some numbers like 1AA, 1BC etc. This is the coding for the organization breakdown structure which will help us to code cost accounts more explicitly.

Description of Roles by integrating WBS and OBS:

By integrating OBS’s most specific roles and WBS’s sub deliverables cost accounts are produced which contains different sets of work packages. Those work packages are then integrated with the responsible person and by completing those work packages deliverables are met. The full integration diagram was difficult to put here, so Integration diagram is posted in parts. The colored boxes are showing the cost accounts and these cost accounts can be assigned codes just like the codes were assigned. By integrating the codes of WBS and OBS code for cost account is obtained like 1BA.1.1.1.1, 1BB.1.1.2 etc. These pictures shows clearly who is responsible for which task.
